About Festival

History

Long before concrete met the mountain, the people of Morena Thesele sang to the clouds, thanking the skies for water that fed their valleys and herds. When the Katse Dam rose, locals decided not to let silence fill the new lake instead, they poured their stories into song, dance, and rhythm. The Katse Dam Festival began as a small gathering of elders, dancers, and storytellers who met by the reservoir’s edge with drums, moropa. They turned the echoes off the giant dam wall into a beating heartbeat, fusing old traditions with modern energy. Each year, the festival grows, drawing visitors into a living legend where the water remembers every song and the mountains keep every secret.
